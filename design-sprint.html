Design Sprint

Design Sprint is an efficient implementation of the Design Thinking philosophy.
It's a proven method to design a new product, project, or feature that can be completed in just a few days.

Why use it?

Risk Reduction

The Design Sprint will tell you if your product, feature or idea is something your customers actually want before you invest your valuable resourses on something that might not produce value.

Results are time-boxed

The Design Sprint is a highly-organized time-boxed set of processes and actions that lead you all the way from an idea to actionable customer validation. Where more traditional approaches can take weeks or months to reveal whether the idea has legs, the Design Sprint provides high quality feedback within days.

Reservoir of ideas

The early stages of a Design Sprint extract many ideas from the participants. In general, all but one of these idea threads is carried all the way through to the end, but the mass of ideas, perspectives and potential solutions is itself a resource of great worth to a business.

Leverages psychology

The Behavioral Sciences have amassed a great deal of knowledge about what makes people tick. The Design Sprint leverages everything we currently know about how to get the best out of people and aims it firmly and squarely at solving a business problem. It even makes them feel good in the process.

Ability to Collaborate

The Design Sprint gets the best from participants as individuals, but it also teaches a lasting lesson in how they can best work with others in the context of a team. It shows that productive competion between ideas is very different from counter-productive competition between individuals.

Maximize ROI

A Design Sprint will tell you whether your idea is a hit or a miss, or if needs some work in a specific direction. Whatever the outcome, you will have that clarity within a few days. This short time is a massive plus, but in addition you will go into the next phase of development with the tools and knowledge necessary to get a far better final outcome than if you had started it in the dark.

How does it work?

Define the right problem

Getting a deeper understanding of your challenge's root causes within the context of your business will help pinpoint the exact problem to rally around.

Create a potential solution

Rapidfire brainstorming will produce a lot of exciting and viable ideas. Your team will select one that has the most promise to create impact on your product.

Test to validate or invalidate the idea

Validating your product concept increases speed to market and lowers chance of failure.

What we provide

For a typical Design Sprint, we reunite a team of 4-6 people with multi-disciplinary backgrounds in order to bring expertise-based knowledge and ideas.

It is a very well-crafted process which takes off most of the pressure from individuals at the same time allowing each of the team member to be heard.

We all work on the same challenge, though every participant has its own outlook and expertise about it. Everyone has a moment to present their knowledge and insights as well as sketch their own solutions. Which are posted on the wall anonymously.

The Design Sprint creates an environment, where each idea, no matter if it comes from a big CEO or an intern has the same weight.

During a Design Sprint we use time efficiently focusing on something that brings value, the ideas which team brought and connecting them into solutions that will make a change.

Our job is to facilitate the process and guide your team through the different phases.

The size of the team and the duration of the sprint can be customized according the client needs.
